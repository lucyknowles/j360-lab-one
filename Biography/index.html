The 1975 are a British alternative rock band. The band is originally from Manchester, England.

Matthew Healy plays guitars and sings vocals, Adam Hann plays guitar, George Daniel on drums and backing vocals, and Ross MacDonald plays bass.

The band all went to Wilmslow High School in Cheshire (south of Manchester) and started playing music together as teenagers in 2002. They have been making music together since they were 15years old.

The name of their band was inspired by notes in an old Beat poetry book by Jack Kerouac dated 'i June, The 1975'.

Their first EP was released in August 2012. It was titled Facedown. It was popular on UK radio, which led them to a UK and Ireland tour. The tour then expanded in 2013 to include multiple US dates.

In September 2013, the band released their first official self named album. They have since been touring worldwide and attended their first major American music and arts festival, Coachella in 2014. Since, they most notably, performed at the Royal Albert Hall and on Saturday night live.
